Short bio

Since September 2025, I am an Assistant Professor (Maître de conférences) at Université Lumière Lyon 2 and a member of LIRIS. From 2024 to 2025, I was a postdoctoral researcher at Mines Saint-Étienne. I earned my PhD in Computer Science from Université Côte d’Azur and hold engineering degrees from ENSAI Rennes and a master Master of Science in Mathematics and Applications Specialization: Pure Mathematics. My research spans deep learning, anomaly detection, computational cost, energy, and sustainable AI.

Research

I develop frugal / Green AI methods to reduce the carbon and energy footprint of machine learning models. I study optimization and compression techniques tailored to resource-constrained settings. In parallel, I work on anomaly and drift detection for time-series data, with applications in health, industry, and the environment.

Topics

  • Efficient deep learning: training/inference cost, energy-aware evaluation, scaling laws
  • Model optimization & compression for constrained deployments
  • Anomaly and drift detection in time series (predictive maintenance, monitoring)
